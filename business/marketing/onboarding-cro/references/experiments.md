# Onboarding CRO Experiment Ideas

Experiment catalog for onboarding flow optimization. Goal: reduce time-to-value and increase activation rate.

---

## Flow Simplification

**Step count**
- Remove one onboarding step at a time (measure completion rate impact)
- Combine two related steps into one screen
- Move non-critical questions to profile settings (collect later)
- "Skip for now" option on each step vs. required completion

**Step ordering**
- Account setup → product setup → invite team vs. product setup first
- Show "aha moment" step earlier in sequence
- Move integrations/connection step to after first value delivery
- Delay billing/upgrade prompts until after first success moment

**Progress indicators**
- Step counter ("Step 2 of 5") vs. progress bar vs. no indicator
- Show estimated time ("~3 minutes") vs. no estimate
- Checklist-style completion tracker visible throughout vs. linear wizard

---

## First-Use Experience

**Welcome screen**
- Personalized welcome ("Welcome, [Name]! Let's set up your [use case]") vs. generic
- Role/goal selection on welcome screen vs. skip to product
- Short video walkthrough vs. text-only welcome
- Interactive demo mode vs. live environment

**First action**
- Guided first action (click here to do X) vs. empty state with suggestion
- Pre-populated example content vs. blank slate
- Template gallery as first step vs. blank creation
- "Quick win" task (something completable in 60 seconds) vs. comprehensive setup

**Product tour**
- Interactive tooltip tour vs. modal overlay tour vs. no tour
- Opt-in tour vs. auto-start tour
- Contextual tooltips (appear when near feature) vs. sequential fixed tour
- Short 3-step tour vs. comprehensive 8-step tour

---

## Progress and Motivation

**Completion mechanics**
- Onboarding checklist in sidebar (persistent) vs. one-time wizard
- Progress percentage visible vs. checklist items only
- Reward/celebration animation on completing steps vs. none
- Email reminder for incomplete onboarding: Day 1 / Day 3 / Day 7

**Gamification**
- Points or badges for completing steps vs. no gamification
- "Power user" tier after completing all steps vs. no tier
- Team leaderboard for setup completion (B2B) vs. individual only

---

## Personalization

**Role-based paths**
- Show different onboarding steps based on role selection vs. single universal flow
- Industry-specific templates auto-selected based on signup data vs. generic templates
- Use case path: different steps for "solo" vs. "team" use

**Goal-based customization**
- Ask primary goal at start, tailor steps to that goal
- Offer 3 pre-set onboarding tracks ("Quick setup", "Full setup", "Team setup")
- Skip advanced steps if user selects basic use case

---

## Support and Help Availability

**In-flow help**
- Live chat proactively triggered during onboarding vs. passive chat icon
- Contextual help tooltip on each field vs. general help link
- Video help icon next to complex steps vs. text help only
- "Get help" CTA during onboarding vs. wait for support request

**Human touchpoints (B2B)**
- Automated email offering setup call: Day 0 vs. Day 2 vs. Day 7
- In-app prompt to book onboarding call vs. email only
- Account manager intro for enterprise: immediate vs. after first login

---

## Email and Notification Sequences

**Activation emails**
- Day 0 welcome email: simple confirmation vs. "here's how to get started" vs. video walkthrough
- Day 1 nudge: specific next step vs. general "come back" vs. no email
- Day 3 check-in: ask if they need help vs. feature highlight
- Day 7 win: show what they accomplished vs. show what they're missing

**Push notifications (mobile)**
- Onboarding reminder notification timing: 24h vs. 48h
- Notification copy: action-oriented ("Finish setup") vs. benefit-oriented ("You're 1 step from your first [result]")
- Notification with deep link to exact step vs. launch to home

---

## Activation Definition Experiments

Sometimes the experiment is defining the right activation event:
- Test which early behavior best predicts 30-day retention
- Test which "first value moment" correlates with conversion to paid
- Identify the "magic number" (e.g., 3 projects created = retained user)
