# AI Writing Detection Reference

Common patterns that signal AI-generated content. Avoid these to pass detection tools and read as authentically human.

---

## Structural Tells

**Overused opening patterns**
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "In the realm of..."
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "It's important to note..."
- "Navigating the complexities of..."
- "When it comes to..."

**Filler transitions**
- "Furthermore" / "Moreover" / "Additionally"
- "In conclusion" / "To summarize" / "In summary"
- "It is essential that" / "It is crucial that"
- "One must consider" / "One should note"

**Overly balanced hedging**
- "While X has advantages, it also has disadvantages"
- "On one hand... On the other hand..."
- Presenting every perspective with equal weight when the content doesn't require it

---

## Punctuation Tells

**Em dash overuse** — AI models default to em dashes for parenthetical asides. Replace with:
- Commas where possible
- Actual parentheses (for genuinely parenthetical information)
- Two separate sentences

**Colon abuse**
- Starting bullet lists with "Here are the key points:"
- "There are three main factors:" before every list

---

## Vocabulary Tells

**Buzzword clustering** — AI tends to stack these together:
- delve, dive deep, robust, leverage, utilize, facilitate, streamline
- comprehensive, holistic, nuanced, multifaceted
- game-changer, paradigm shift, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art
- foster, cultivate, harness, unlock potential

**Vague intensifiers**
- "truly", "certainly", "undoubtedly", "absolutely"
- "a wide range of", "a variety of", "numerous"
- "significant", "substantial", "considerable" without specifics

---

## Content Structure Tells

**The 3-bullet summary trap** — AI defaults to exactly 3 items in lists. Vary list lengths: 2, 4, 5, 7.

**Perfect symmetry** — Human writing has natural asymmetry. If every section is exactly the same length and depth, it signals AI.

**Missing specifics** — Vague references to "studies", "experts", "research" without citations. Replace with:
- Named studies with dates
- Specific data points
- Real examples with company/person names

**Hedged everything** — AI hedges claims excessively. Confident claims signal human authorship.

---

## Sentence-Level Tells

**Passive voice overuse**
- "It can be seen that..." → "The data shows..."
- "It has been found that..." → "Researchers found..."
- "Steps should be taken to..." → "Take these steps:"

**Uniform sentence length** — Mix short punchy sentences with longer complex ones. AI produces uniform medium-length sentences.

**Missing contractions** — AI formal writing avoids contractions. "You will find" → "You'll find". "It is" → "It's".

---

## Detection Heuristics

| Signal | Human | AI |
|--------|-------|-----|
| Sentence length variation | High | Low |
| Specific examples | Named, verifiable | Generic, vague |
| Opinion statements | Clear stance | Balanced to a fault |
| Vocabulary rarity | Occasional unusual words | Buzzword clusters |
| Structural symmetry | Asymmetric | Perfectly parallel |
| Personal pronouns | "I", "we", "our team" | Avoided or generic |
